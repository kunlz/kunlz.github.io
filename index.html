About me

I'm currently working at the Institute of Tibetan Plateau Research, Chinese Academy of Sciences (ITPCAS) as a post-doctoral researcher. I received my Ph.D. from Lanzhou University in 2018, and B.S. from Northwest A&F University in 2012. I also worked as a joint Ph.D student in the NCGRT of Flinders University in 2017.

My research interests mainly focus on the terrestrial water cycle and remote sensing, especially in the global evapotranspiration modeling and soil moisture. Furthermore, My research mainly involves the use of Bayesian approachs and other statistical methods to investigate the sensitivity and optimization in model parameters. My latest project focuses on the soil water balance and try to estimate the total amount of irrigation water use based on the soil moisture and precipitation from satellite at global scale.
